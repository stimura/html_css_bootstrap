Latitude vs. Cloudiness:

My analysis into the relationship between world cities latitudes and average cloudiness of the city during the year, resulted a conclusion that I found a bit surprising. In terms of a relationship, in a linear sense, there is none. The slope of the best fit line, seen in the chart on the right, has a slope very close to zero. A slope of zero usually indicates a relationship that is non-existent. But after a closer examination, there seems to be some relationship. Since at every 20% addition of cloudiness, an apparent straight line forms among the range of latitudes. This relationship is not captured linearly, but could be described in a non-linear statistical measure. I am looking into this possibility, and will update this page as soon as I get an answer.
